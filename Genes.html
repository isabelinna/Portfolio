Analysing the Genetic Links to Colon Cancer

In this project, I utilized R programming and a database of genes associated with colon cancer to uncover meaningful insights. After retrieving the relevant gene data and expression values, I performed data normalization to ensure accurate analysis. By selecting specific samples from patients in different stages of cancer, I conducted statistical tests, such as t-tests, to identify genes with significant associations. Applying filters based on significance levels and effect sizes, I extracted a subset of genes that exhibited strong relationships with colon cancer.

To visualize the results, I generated various graphical representations, including heatmaps, scatter plots, R-I plots, and volcano plots. These visualizations helped depict the gene expression patterns, significant gene clusters, and potential biomarkers associated with colon cancer. By combining statistical analysis and data visualization techniques, this project contributed to our understanding of the genetic landscape of colon cancer and provided valuable insights for future research and clinical applications. Thanks to this project, I now have some experience in bioinformatics and data science.
